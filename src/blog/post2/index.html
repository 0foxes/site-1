Hidden Gems - 6 Amazing Apps for Your Phone

31/03/2020

(First, a quick note about my plans for the blog: I’m planning to post at least once a week, and I expect my articles to be around 750-1250 words each. Most times they’ll be about technology, science, or coding, but I’ll occasionally post articles on other topics. Enjoy!)

Nowadays, most people have a smartphone. Though most smartphones already come with pre-installed apps that let you call, text, play music, and browse the internet, almost everyone chooses to install more apps on their phone to either add additional functionality, change the look of their phone, or pass the time with a game or two. With app store charts, “best of” articles, and word of mouth, it’s not hard to learn about the most popular apps. This is a list of amazing, useful, lesser-known apps for Android and iOS (sorry if you run Samsung Tizen, Windows Mobile, or another Linux-based OS). After all, it’s the hidden gems that are really worth digging for.

IFTTT(Free)

Maybe you want to create some custom commands for your voice assistant. Maybe you want to turn on your WiFi automatically when you get home. Maybe you want to know whenever the Dow falls because of a global pandemic. Maybe you want to link all your different social media accounts together. IFTTT lets you do that—and more. It lets you connect and control your different smart devices, services, and social media. If you can think of something, you can probably automate it using one of their “applets”. IFTTT stands for “IF This, Then That” and that’s exactly how it works.

Nova Launcher (Free With Premium Tier)

Your phone’s app launcher is the one app that “launches” other apps. It controls how your home screen, app screens, and app icons look and function. Unfortunately, you can only change your app launcher on Android (possibly because each phone company has its own launcher) but Nova Launcher goes above and beyond what a normal launcher does. It's arguably the best Android launcher, with loads of features, fast performance, and regular updates. You can customize everything, change the way your app icons look, backup and restore your home screen layout, and even add animations! With a little tinkering, you can give your phone a complete makeover with Nova Launcher.

Bear (Free With Premium Tier)

To be fair to any iOS users out there who feel like they’ve been forgotten, here’s an iOS-exclusive app. Bear is a unique writing and note-taking app that lets you jot down (using your stylus) quick notes, type out your ideas, doodle, and write code (it supports over 150 programming languages). It comes with full support for Markdown formatting, allows you to link between notes to better organize your ideas, has hashtag support for easy searching, works as a simple to-do list, and includes numerous themes to customize the appearance. Bear even automatically syncs your notes between multiple devices, so it’s easy to see why many people consider this the best writing app on iOS.

Discord (Free)

Discord bills itself as the best messaging app for gamers, but it may very well be the best messaging platform out there. It supports all the major PC and mobile operating systems and has a browser client for the more obscure ones. Discord is mainly organized into “servers”, communities of people who interact with each other (like a gaming group, your class in school, or a worldwide art community). In those servers, there are text and voice “channels”, areas dedicated to a certain topic (an art server may have #watercolours, #digital-art, and #sketches). You can also video chat with other people and share your desktop in real-time. On top of all this, there are no ads at all. Now you can socially distance while still staying connected!

Tool Box (Free)

Our smartphones are jam-packed with a wide array of sensors, but most people don’t even know they’re there. These two apps (an Android and an iOS version) are the swiss army knife of apps, since each is actually a collection of multiple tools your phone can be. It lets your phone act like a compass, level, ruler, distance metre (point your phone at something, and it tells you how far away it is), metal detector, altimeter, heart rate monitor, barcode scanner, tuner, and many more tools. You may not be able to use all the tools at once, but all of them are incredibly useful.

Snapseed (Free)

You’re probably not going to create any photoshop masterpieces on mobile, but quickly touching up your photo on your phone is infinitely more convenient than exporting it to a computer, editing it in photoshop, then saving it onto your phone again. Snapseed is a modern photo editor by Google with a minimalist, “material” design. It comes with 29 tools and filters such as cropping, rotation, blurring, adding text, perspective, HDR, exposure, white balance, and details. It comes with even more “styles” you can tweak to make your photo better! It comes with support for a variety of different image file formats, including .jpg, .png, and .raw.
