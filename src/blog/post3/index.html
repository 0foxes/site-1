Barely Afloat - The Royal Canadian Navy in 2020

08/04/2020

Disregarding Trump’s newly formed US Space Force, there are three ways a country may be attacked or its sovereignty challenged: by land, air, or sea. Despite Canada being at peace today, there have been times in the past when Canadians have had to wage war, either in faraway lands or closer to home. Since Canada is surrounded by three oceans and only borders the US by land (they haven’t tried to invade us since 1812), this burden mainly falls on the air force and the navy. As aircraft ranges are extremely limited (the CF-18 (our primary fighter) has a maximum combat range of just above 500km), Canada’s defence primarily falls to its Navy. In this post, I’m going to be talking about the history, current mission, and future plans of the Royal Canadian Navy (RCN).
